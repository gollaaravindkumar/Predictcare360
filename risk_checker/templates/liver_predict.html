⚠️ Important Information for High-Risk Patients

Liver disease affects your liver’s ability to process nutrients and detoxify the body. Early detection is crucial.

  • Jaundice (yellowing of skin and eyes)
  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Abdominal pain and swelling
  • Dark urine and pale stools
  • Nausea or vomiting

  • Excessive alcohol consumption
  • Obesity or diabetes
  • Hepatitis B or C infection
  • Family history of liver disease
  • Medications affecting liver function

  • Maintain a balanced diet and healthy weight
  • Limit alcohol and avoid smoking
  • Exercise regularly
  • Get vaccinated for hepatitis B
  • Have regular liver function checkups
Consult a Hepatologist
