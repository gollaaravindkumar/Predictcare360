⚠️ Important Information for High-Risk Patients

Heart disease refers to conditions that affect the heart’s structure and function, including coronary artery disease, heart attack, and heart failure. Early intervention can prevent complications.

  • Chest pain or discomfort
  • Shortness of breath
  • Fatigue or dizziness
  • Swelling in legs or ankles
  • Irregular heartbeat

  • High blood pressure or cholesterol
  • Smoking and alcohol use
  • Obesity and physical inactivity
  • Diabetes
  • Family history of heart disease

  • Eat a heart-healthy diet rich in fruits and vegetables
  • Exercise regularly — at least 30 minutes daily
  • Manage blood pressure, cholesterol, and blood sugar levels
  • Quit smoking and limit alcohol intake
  • Reduce stress through meditation or yoga

Healthy Living Recommendations
1. Eat Smart:
  • Consume more fruits, vegetables, and whole grains.
  • Limit saturated fats, sugar, and processed foods.
2. Stay Active:
  • Exercise at least 30 minutes daily (walking, cycling).
  • Include both cardio and strength exercises.
3. Monitor Health:
  • Check blood pressure, cholesterol, and heart rate regularly.
  • Have annual health check-ups.
4. Sleep & Stress:
  • Get 7–8 hours of sleep nightly.
  • Practice meditation or yoga to manage stress.
5. Avoid Risks:
  • Avoid smoking and limit alcohol consumption.
