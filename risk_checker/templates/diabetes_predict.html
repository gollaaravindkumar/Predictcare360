⚠️ Important Information for High-Risk Patients

Diabetes is a chronic condition where blood sugar levels remain high due to the body's inability to produce or use insulin effectively.

  • Frequent urination
  • Excessive thirst or hunger
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Fatigue or weakness
  • Blurred vision
  • Slow-healing sores

  • Family history of diabetes
  • Overweight or obesity
  • Physical inactivity
  • Unhealthy diet
  • High blood pressure or cholesterol
  • Gestational diabetes history

  • Maintain a balanced diet low in sugar and refined carbs
  • Engage in regular physical activity
  • Monitor blood sugar levels regularly
  • Quit smoking and limit alcohol
  • Manage stress effectively

Healthy Living Recommendations
1. Eat Smart:
  • Consume more fiber-rich foods (vegetables, whole grains).
  • Limit sugary drinks and processed foods.
2. Stay Active:
  • Exercise for at least 30 minutes daily (walking, cycling).
  • Include both cardio and strength exercises.
3. Monitor Health:
  • Check blood glucose, BP, and cholesterol regularly.
  • Have annual health check-ups.
4. Sleep & Stress:
  • Get 7–8 hours of sleep.
  • Practice meditation or yoga to manage stress.
5. Avoid Risks:
  • Avoid smoking and reduce alcohol consumption.
