⚠️ Important Information for High-Risk Patients

Breast cancer occurs when abnormal breast cells grow uncontrollably and form tumors. Early detection is crucial, as invasive cancers can spread to lymph nodes or other organs.

  • A lump or thickening in the breast
  • Changes in breast shape, size, or skin texture
  • Abnormal nipple discharge
  • Skin dimpling, redness, or swelling

  • Family history of breast cancer
  • Hormonal and genetic factors
  • Obesity, alcohol, and smoking
  • Radiation exposure or late menopause

  • Maintain a healthy diet and body weight
  • Regular exercise and screenings
  • Avoid alcohol and tobacco
  • Consult your doctor for early intervention
